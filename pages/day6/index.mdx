# Day 6 — Binary Trees and BSTs

Today we move from linear data (arrays, lists, stacks, queues) to **hierarchical data**. Trees are the structure behind file systems, syntax parsers, expression evaluators, decision processes — anything where one thing branches into many.

We'll focus on the workhorse of the family: the **binary tree** (every node has at most two children) and its sorted variant, the **Binary Search Tree (BST)**.

## What you'll learn today

- The vocabulary of trees — root, leaf, depth, height, ancestor, descendant
- Common **binary tree types**: full, complete, perfect, balanced, BST, AVL, heap
- How a **BST** keeps elements sorted automatically and gives you O(log n) search
- The three **traversal orders** every interview asks about: inorder, preorder, postorder
- Four classic problems to lock in the recursion-on-trees pattern

<Callout type="info">
    Trees are where recursion really shines. Almost every tree operation has a clean recursive solution: "do something with this node, then recursively do it for the left and right subtrees." Day 5 prepared you for this.
</Callout>
