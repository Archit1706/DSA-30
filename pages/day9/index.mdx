## What you'll learn here

- What a **graph** is, and the difference between **directed / undirected** and **weighted / unweighted**
- The vocabulary: **vertices, edges, degree, path, cycle, connected component, DAG**
- Two ways to represent a graph in code: **adjacency list** vs **adjacency matrix** — and when each one wins
- Special graph shapes: **trees, DAGs, bipartite graphs, complete graphs**
- Building a graph in C++/Python/Java and printing its structure

This is the foundation chapter. [Day 10](/day10) covers DFS and BFS — the two universal graph traversal algorithms. [Day 21](/day21) covers shortest paths. [Day 22](/day22) covers MSTs. [Day 23](/day23) covers topological sort. All of them assume you've built the mental model that this chapter lays down.

Graphs are everywhere once you start looking: social networks, road maps, web pages and links, dependencies between software modules, electrical circuits, biology networks. Half of "real-world algorithm problems" reduce to a graph problem in disguise.
